Advanced Battery Modelling

Our thesis is as follows:

  1. Battery sales have been increasing 33% year on year for the last 30 years and with the rise of EVs and Autonomous Embodied Systems this trend is likely to continue well into the next 20 years.
  2. Deep learning is highly appropriate for modelling complex, non-linear systems like battery chemistry.

To prove our thesis we have developed a machine learning algorithm capable of accurately estimating the Remaining Useful Life of LFP/graphite cells

On average using 15 cycles of cycling data we can predict RUL within 133 cycles which is a significant improvement over non-deep learning approaches, which require 100 cycles worth of data to achieve similar performance.

We are currently working on securing a joint pilot project with an existing manufacturers from industry to demonstrate the capabilities of machine learning in the battery EOL estimation space at a commercial scale.
